## The Market Right Now

Three tiers of AI agent agencies exist in 2026:

**Tier 1: Prompt Engineers**
They know how to talk to ChatGPT. They'll build you a chatbot with a custom system prompt. It'll work for demos, break in production, and require constant babysitting.

*Cost: $500-5,000*
*Quality: Low*

**Tier 2: Integration Specialists**
They connect AI to your systems. Real APIs, real data, real workflows. But their AI sophistication is basicâ€”think Zapier with GPT bolted on.

*Cost: $5,000-25,000*
*Quality: Medium*

**Tier 3: AI Engineering Firms**
They build robust agent systems. Custom tooling, error handling, monitoring, edge cases. They understand both AI and software engineering.

*Cost: $25,000-100,000+*
*Quality: High*

## Questions to Ask in Discovery

**Technical:**
1. What AI platform/models do you recommend and why?
2. How do you handle testing AI agents before production?
3. What's your approach to security and data privacy?
4. How do you monitor agent performance over time?
5. What happens when the AI makes a mistake?

**Process:**
1. What's your timeline for this type of project?
2. Who from our team needs to be involved?
3. How do you handle scope changes?
4. What does ongoing support look like?

**Business:**
1. Can you share 3 references with similar projects?
2. What's included vs. what's extra?
3. What are the typical failure modes for projects like this?
4. How do you measure success?

## Structuring the Engagement

### Phase 1: Discovery (1-2 weeks)

Agency learns your business, systems, and goals. Deliverable: Technical proposal with architecture, timeline, pricing.

*Pay: Fixed fee or hourly*

### Phase 2: Build (4-12 weeks)

Agency builds the agent system. Regular check-ins. Working prototype midway.

*Pay: Milestone-based*

### Phase 3: Deploy + Tune (2-4 weeks)

Launch to production. Monitor. Fix issues. Tune performance.

*Pay: Included in build or small additional fee*

### Phase 4: Ongoing Support (Monthly)

Maintenance, monitoring, updates, improvements.

*Pay: Monthly retainer (typically 10-20% of build cost)*
